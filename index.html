About the Leyat Helica

The Leyat Helica was a revolutionary French automobile manufactured between 1919 and 1925 by Marcel Leyat. Unlike traditional cars, it was propelled by a giant front-mounted propeller powered by an 8 bhp Scorpion engine. With its lightweight plywood body, the Helica weighed just 250 kg (550 lb), making it capable of reaching impressive speeds for its time. Passengers sat behind each other as in an aircraft, emphasizing its aerodynamic design.

History of the Helica

Marcel Leyat, an engineer and aviation pioneer born in 1885, envisioned a vehicle that combined aeronautical principles with automotive design. After graduating from the École Centrale Paris in 1907, he worked on various aircraft projects before introducing the Helica in 1919. Over six years, Leyat produced approximately 30 vehicles, continually experimenting with different configurations. In 1927, a Helica set a speed record of 106 mph (171 km/h) at the Montlhéry circuit, showcasing its remarkable performance.
